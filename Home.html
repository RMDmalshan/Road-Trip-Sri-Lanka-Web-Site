Sigiriya is a historic rock stronghold in Dambulla, Matale, Central Sri Lanka, that is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Climbing the massive rock formation's 1200 stairs for a stunning perspective of the lush jungle beneath is one of the greatest things to do there.

The next location in this list is one of the most interesting spots in Sri Lanka, and it is incredibly distinctive and lovely in every way. We're discussing the Temple of the Tooth Relic. This temple is quite popular, and most tourists visiting Sri Lanka come here primarily for its uniqueness. Yala National Park is the place to go if you want to go on a safari in Sri Lanka. The enormous park, which is full of forests and wildlife and is a sanctuary for elephants, crocodiles, leopards, and hundreds of different bird species, is located in southeast Sri Lanka on the edge of the Indian Ocean.

Mirissa is the major fishing port on the island's south shore, located on the island's southernmost point. It's not difficult to get to because of its beauty and proximity to Colombo (the capital city). The capital's airport is about a two-hour drive away. The small beach town and its vast beach are both lovely, with a dense grove of palm trees surrounding them. The settlement is located on a little hill behind the beach.
